Making pasta at home

Homemade Pasta Dough:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Form a Mound:
    • On a clean surface, pour the flour and make a mound with a well in the center.
  2. Add Eggs and Salt:
    • Crack the eggs into the well and add the salt.
  3. Mix Gradually:
    • Using a fork, gradually mix the flour into the eggs until a dough starts to form.
  4. Knead the Dough:
    • Use your hands to knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic. If it's too sticky, add a little more flour.
  5. Rest the Dough:
    • Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes. This allows the gluten to relax and makes the dough easier to roll.
  6. Roll the Dough:
    • Cut the dough into manageable portions and roll each portion into a thin sheet using a rolling pin or pasta machine.
  7. Cut Pasta Shapes:
    • Once rolled, cut the pasta into your desired shapes—fettuccine, tagliatelle, or even simple squares for homemade ravioli.
  8. Cook the Pasta:
    • Boil a pot of salted water and cook the fresh pasta for 2-4 minutes or until al dente. Fresh pasta cooks faster than dried pasta.

Simple Tomato Sauce:

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Sauté Garlic:
    • In a pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant.
  2. Add Crushed Tomatoes:
    • Pour in the crushed tomatoes, and season with salt and pepper. Stir well.
  3. Simmer the Sauce:
    • Let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly.
  4. Add Fresh Basil (Optional):
    • If you have fresh basil, chop it and add it to the sauce just before serving for added flavor.

Serving:

  1. Cook the Pasta:
    • Boil the fresh pasta in salted water until it floats to the top, indicating it's cooked.
  2. Drain and Plate:
    • Drain the pasta and plate it. Top with the homemade tomato sauce.
  3. Optional Toppings:
    • Grate Parmesan cheese over the top, and add a drizzle of olive oil.
  4. Garnish (Optional):
    • Garnish with fresh basil leaves for a burst of color and flavor.
